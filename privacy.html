1. Overview

Flickr ("we," "our," "the site") is a free movie discovery tool. This policy explains what information is collected when you use flickr-site.vercel.app, how it's used, and what control you have over it. We've tried to write it in plain language rather than dense legal boilerplate — if anything is unclear, reach out using the contact details at the bottom of this page.

The short version: Flickr doesn't require an account, doesn't ask for personal details, and stores your watchlist only in your own browser. The main privacy-relevant activity on the site comes from the third-party services we rely on — movie data, video embeds, and advertising — which are detailed below.

2. Information we collect

We do not operate user accounts, logins, or sign-up forms, so we don't collect names, email addresses, or payment details through the site itself.

Like virtually any website, our hosting provider and content-delivery network automatically log basic technical information for security and performance purposes, such as:

  • IP address (used transiently for request routing and abuse prevention)
  • Browser type and device/operating system
  • Pages visited and approximate time spent
  • Referring URL (the page that linked you here)

This information is standard server/CDN log data and is not used by us to build an individual profile of you.

3. Your watchlist & local storage

When you add a movie to your watchlist, that list is saved using your browser's local storage — a small amount of data kept on your own device. It is never transmitted to our servers, and we never see or have access to it.

Because it lives in your browser, your watchlist is specific to the device and browser you used to create it. Clearing your browser's site data, using a different browser, or switching devices will not carry it over. Your selected language and theme (light/dark) preference are stored the same way.

4. Third-party services

The Movie Database (TMDB). Movie titles, posters, cast lists, ratings, and descriptions are fetched live from TMDB's public API. Your search terms are sent to TMDB's servers in order to return results; this use is subject to TMDB's own privacy policy. Flickr is not endorsed or certified by TMDB.

YouTube (trailers). When you press play on a trailer, an embedded YouTube player loads, which is operated by Google. That embed may set its own cookies and collect viewing data in line with Google's privacy policy. No trailer or video data loads until you actively click play.

Streaming provider links. The "where to watch" links on each movie take you to the relevant platform's own search page (Netflix, Prime Video, Disney+, etc.). Once you leave Flickr, that platform's own privacy policy applies.

5. Cookies & advertising

Flickr displays ads through Google AdSense. Google and its advertising partners may use cookies, device identifiers, and similar technologies to serve ads based on your prior visits to this and other websites, and to measure ad performance.

Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visits to this site and/or other sites on the Internet. You can opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google's Ads Settings, or opt out of a third-party vendor's use of cookies for personalized advertising by visiting www.aboutads.info.

We do not control the specific cookies that AdSense sets, and recommend reviewing Google's own disclosures for the most current detail on what's collected and how it's used.

6. Your choices & controls

  • Clear your watchlist and language/theme preferences at any time by clearing this site's data in your browser settings.
  • Use your browser's cookie settings or a content blocker to limit third-party cookies from ads or embedded video.
  • Opt out of personalized advertising via the Google links in Section 5.
  • If you are located somewhere with statutory data rights (for example under GDPR or CCPA/CPRA) and believe we hold personal data about you beyond what's described here, contact us using the details in Section 10 and we'll respond as required by applicable law.

7. Children's privacy

Flickr is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Some content discoverable through the site (movie titles, trailers) may carry mature ratings, consistent with the third-party sources it's pulled from; parental discretion applies as it would with any film database or streaming guide.

8. Data retention & security

Because we don't operate accounts or store personal data on our own servers, there is little of ours to secure beyond standard hosting-provider protections (HTTPS encryption in transit, provider-level infrastructure security). Data you store locally in your browser (watchlist, preferences) remains under your control and is retained only as long as your browser keeps it.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time to reflect changes in the services we use or in applicable law. The "last updated" date at the top of this page will always reflect the most recent revision. Material changes will be reflected here directly; we encourage checking back periodically.

10. Contact us

Questions about this policy or how Flickr handles data can be sent to hello@flickr-site.vercel.app.
