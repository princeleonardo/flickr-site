About & Contact

Who's behind Flickr, why it exists, and how to reach us.

the short version

Flickr is a small, independently-run movie discovery site. There's no studio behind it, no subscription, and no catalogue of pirated streams — just a fast, clean way to search what's out there, see trailers and ratings in one place, and keep a running list of what you want to watch next.

It started as a personal itch: most "discovery" tools these days are either bloated with autoplay ads and fake download buttons, or they're a wall of posters with no context. We wanted something closer to flipping through a well-organized shelf — genre by genre, trailer on hand, one click to the platform that actually has it.

what we're not
  • We don't host, stream, or link to pirated video files. Every "where to watch" link points to the legitimate platform (Netflix, Prime Video, Disney+, and so on) via its official listing.
  • We don't require an account, an email address, or a credit card to use the search or the watchlist.
  • We don't sell your data — because we don't collect enough of it to sell. See the privacy policy for the specifics.
how it's built

Movie data, posters, cast info, and trailers are pulled live from The Movie Database (TMDB), a community-maintained film database. Flickr isn't endorsed or certified by TMDB — we're just a client of their public API, same as thousands of other apps.

Your watchlist lives entirely in your own browser's local storage. It never touches a server, which also means it's tied to this device and browser — clearing your browser data will clear it too.
